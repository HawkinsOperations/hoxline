## Business Problem

Security teams often need proof-boundary control, not more confident AI output. The HYPOTHESIS_ONLY problem statement is that AI-assisted work creates review load when claims, evidence, and authority are not separated.

Hoxline is positioned as a product and service wedge for that review load.

## Service Wedge

HYPOTHESIS_ONLY: Public Proof Hardening / ProofOps readiness review could be the first service wedge.

The review would inspect whether an artifact has:

- authority source references,
- allowed and blocked claim wording,
- evidence-state labels,
- human review gates,
- non-claim lists,
- public release boundaries.

This is a hypothesis, not a customer offer and not a legal availability claim.

## Software Wedge

HYPOTHESIS_ONLY: Hoxline could grow into reusable software surfaces:

- policy packs,
- evidence graph examples,
- ProofCards,
- Gauntlet runs,
- Claim Authority reports,
- promotion-state guardrails.

The HO-DET-001 bridge is the first bounded reviewer example of this software wedge.

## Buyer Pain Hypothesis

HYPOTHESIS_ONLY: The buyer pain is not a lack of generated text. The pain is uncertainty about what can be safely claimed after AI-assisted security work produces a plausible result.

Hoxline addresses that pain by forcing each claim through proof ceiling, source reference, required evidence, and human review status.
